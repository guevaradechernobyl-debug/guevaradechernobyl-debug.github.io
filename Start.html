WHY THIS BLOG EXIST

This wasn’t supposed to be serious.

It started as a school project. Just another requirement. Just another grade.

But somewhere between fixing broken code, debugging errors at midnight, and finally understanding how the logic actually worked… something clicked.

There’s a strange kind of euphoria in solving a problem that once made no sense. In turning imagination into something functional. In watching variables, conditions, and lines of code come together like they were always meant to.

What began as homework slowly became obsession.

So I added more features.
Then more.
Then I decided to publish it.

Now it lives here — on the internet, publicly. And this time, it’s not for a grade.
