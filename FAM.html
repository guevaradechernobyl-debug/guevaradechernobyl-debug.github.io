IN PROGRESS

This wasn’t supposed to be serious.

It was just a school project. A requirement. Something to finish, submit, and forget about five minutes later.

But somewhere between broken code, error messages that might as well have been written in ancient hieroglyphs, and debugging at hours when normal people are asleep… something changed.

There’s a strange kind of satisfaction in solving a problem that once made you question your intelligence. One moment the code is a mess. The next moment it works like it finally decided to cooperate.

Turns out watching logic fall into place is... addictive.

So what started as homework slowly turned into something else. Curiosity. Then obsession.
